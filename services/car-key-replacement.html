Key Types We Replace

Standard Keys, Chip Keys, Fobs, and Smart Keys

Modern vehicles use different types of keys, and each one requires specific tools and knowledge to replace properly. Here are the main types we handle:

  • Standard metal keys - Traditional cut keys for older vehicles. Fast to duplicate and cut on site.
  • Transponder chip keys - Keys with an embedded electronic chip that must be programmed to match your vehicle's immobilizer.
  • Key fobs and remotes - Wireless remotes that lock, unlock, and sometimes start your vehicle remotely.
  • Flip keys - Folding keys with a metal blade that flips out of the fob housing.
  • Smart proximity keys - Push-to-start keys that unlock and start the car when you are nearby without inserting a key.
